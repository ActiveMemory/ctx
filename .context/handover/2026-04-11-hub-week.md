### Near-term (unblocks the identity-layer MVP)

The security audit has 5 Critical findings. Of them,
four are documented but not yet fixed:

- **H-01**: server-side TLS on the gRPC server. The
  hub calls `grpc.NewServer()` with no credentials.
- **H-02**: client-side TLS. Three sites use
  `insecure.NewCredentials()` unconditionally:
  `internal/hub/client.go:30`,
  `internal/hub/sync_helper.go:28`,
  `internal/hub/failover.go:34`. Until this ships,
  the "nginx reverse proxy" recommendation in
  `hub-multi-machine.md` is un-implementable. **Update
  that recipe when H-02 lands.**
- **H-10 + H-11**: Raft transport is unauthenticated
  and unencrypted. `raft.NewTCPTransport` in
  `cluster.go:61`.
- **H-13**: followers trust master content on
  replication without re-validating. `sync_helper.go:60-76`.

The fifth critical (H-22, Author spoofing) is
**partially resolved**: the schema has the right shape
now, but H-22a (server-authoritative Origin stamping)
still needs to be implemented. This is the cheapest
security fix in the whole audit — 5-line change in
`handler.go publish()`.

H-04 in the audit is the same as H-22a — both are
"server-enforce attribution on publish." When you
work on this, fix Origin and (future) UserID in the
same commit. They share the `authFromContext` plumbing
that doesn't exist yet but is the right shape
(`validateBearer` attaches `ClientInfo` to context,
handler reads it back).
